The most important fiscal obligations of Romanian citizens are:
-income tax;
- local taxes and fees (such as the tax on buildings, the tax and the tax on owned land or taxes on means of transport);
- tax for advertising and publicity services, tax for display for the purpose of advertising and publicity, tax on shows, special tax for tourist promotion of the city of Bucharest;
-other taxes and property taxes (agricultural tax, tax for concessioned land, stamp duties on successions and other stamp duties).

In Romania, income tax is paid by the following categories of taxpayers:
- resident natural persons;
- non-resident natural persons carrying out an independent activity through a permanent establishment in Romania;
- non-resident natural persons carrying out dependent activities in Romania;
- non-resident natural persons who obtain income provided for in art. 129 of Law 227/2015 on the Fiscal Code with subsequent amendments and additions.

Taxable income categories
Taxable income represents the category of income subject to taxation and includes both income in money and the equivalent in lei of income in kind. According to Law 227/2015 on the Fiscal Code with subsequent amendments and additions, these can be:
income from independent activities, defined according to art. 67;
income from wages and wages, defined according to art. 76;
income from the transfer of the use of goods, defined according to art. 83;
income from investments, defined according to art. 91;
income from pensions, defined according to art. 99;
income from agricultural activities, forestry and fish farming, defined according to art. 103;
income from prizes and games of chance, defined according to art. 108;
income from the transfer of real estate properties, defined according to art. 111;
income from other sources, defined according to art. 114 and art. 117.
